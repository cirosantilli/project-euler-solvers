# Collatz Prefix Families

This solution models the non‑power‑of‑two Collatz prefix as a sequence of odd
terms with interleaved even runs. A prefix family is determined by the relative
order of all terms; for long prefixes the ordering is governed by the linear
coefficients in the closed form expressions, while for small `q` the constants
still matter and can flip some comparisons.

Key ideas:
- A prefix of length `m` corresponds to a sequence of “odd steps” with associated
  powers of two (the `d` values). For most sequences, the induced ordering is the
  same as the asymptotic ordering; this yields the Fibonacci baseline.
- Ambiguities only arise when the ordering for the minimal valid `q` differs
  from the asymptotic ordering. These are counted by reverse‑generating valid
  `d` lists from the terminating odd `(2^q-1)/3` and comparing two orderings.
- The asymptotic ordering is computed by sorting the coefficients of `2^q`
  (with a constant tie‑break) without needing huge integers for large `q`.

The program computes:
1. The Fibonacci baseline `F_m`.
2. The number of ambiguous `d` lists (for small even `q`) and adds them.

Run `main.py` to print `f(90)`. The file also includes asserts for the provided
sample values.
